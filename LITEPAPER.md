# Prism Litepaper

**AI Transformation for Human-Agent Work**  
*May 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

Enterprise leaders know AI matters. What they often lack is a practical way to move from pressure and experimentation to ownership of how work and the workforce change.

The answer is **not** another tool rollout.  
**Tool adoption gives people access to AI. Transformation changes how work is designed, governed, and executed.**

Prism helps enterprise leaders turn AI pressure into a practical operating model for human-agent work. Prism helps enterprises become **agentic AI-native** by redesigning work around hybrid teams of humans and AI agents.

The Prism method begins with a practical starter exercise that builds leaders' agent fluency, creates a first governed agent, and tests whether Prism and the organization are a fit. From there, Prism helps leaders identify bottlenecks, redesign functions as hybrid teams, define the **Agent Resources** capability, and establish progressive governance that scales.

Prism is built for leaders who want to **understand agents, redesign work, and take ownership** of how their workforce evolves.

---

## The Pressure: AI Transformation Has Become a Leadership Test

Enterprise leaders are surrounded by messages that they are already behind. The result is pressure, confusion, and the feeling that the AI era is happening *to* them rather than a transition they can deliberately lead.

This pressure often leads to reactive behavior: buying tools, running pilots, or asking teams to “use AI” without changing how work actually gets done. These efforts create activity but rarely create an AI-native organization.

**The real leadership test is not whether an organization has access to AI — it is whether its leaders can turn that access into a redesigned system of work.**

---

## Why Tool Adoption Is Not Transformation

Most organizations have already started adopting AI tools. While these efforts provide value, they fall short of true transformation.

Tool adoption improves isolated tasks but does **not**:
- Redesign how work flows through the organization
- Change roles or decision rights
- Reallocate work between humans and agents
- Create proper agent governance

**Tool adoption gives people access to AI. Transformation changes how work is designed, governed, and executed.**

---

## The Bottleneck Problem: Humans as Workflow Connectors

Legacy processes often use humans as connectors — moving data, chasing approvals, routing work, and reconciling outputs.

> **Humans are not the bottleneck by nature. Legacy process design turns them into bottlenecks.**

Agents change this equation. They can operate continuously across systems, maintain context, and handle repeatable coordination — freeing humans for judgment, creativity, empathy, and leadership.

Prism helps organizations identify these bottlenecks and redesign workflows for hybrid human-agent teams.

---

## The Prism Method: From Leader Fluency to Bottleneck Diagnosis

Prism starts with **executive agent fluency** — a guided exercise where leaders build, register, test, and govern their first personal agent.

This creates shared understanding and serves as the first governed agent in the organization. It is followed by a mutual fit decision and an agentic bottleneck diagnostic.

---

## Hybrid Teams and the Agent Resources Function

Agentic AI-native organizations operate as **hybrid workforces** — humans and agents working as teammates.

Humans focus on judgment, trust, empathy, and leadership.  
Agents handle repeatable coordination, monitoring, and execution.

This requires a new organizational capability: **Agent Resources** — the equivalent of Human Resources but for AI agents (onboarding, permissions, governance, monitoring, retirement, etc.).

Prism helps organizations build this capability.

---

## Progressive Agent Governance

To prevent agent sprawl, Prism introduces **progressive governance**:

- Lightweight agent registry/repository
- Initial governance guidelines
- Observer/escalator agent for monitoring
- Agent Composition Workshops to promote reusability

> **You cannot govern agents you cannot find.**

Governance grows with adoption rather than being imposed too early.

---

## Conclusion: Leaders Must Own How Work Changes

AI transformation is not something that happens *to* leaders. It happens when leaders take ownership of redesigning work and the workforce.

Prism exists to help those leaders begin — by building fluency, identifying bottlenecks, redesigning functions around hybrid teams, establishing Agent Resources, and implementing scalable governance.

**The companies that win will be those whose leaders understand agents and deliberately shape the future of work.**
